India, S377, and Homosexual Identity

WRITER Simon Rodrigues
DESIGNER Kimberly Bager

In this article, Eleanor Brooks recounts her interview with Dakshnamoarthy Natarajan, a homosexual Indian man, as a means to discuss homosexual identity and gay rights in India. She retells parts of D’s experience in his own words, focusing on his to personal journey to acknowledge his gay identity in a country where homosexuality was both illegal and socially rejected. The piece uses the recent repeal of Section 377 in India’s penal code as a point of inflection to discuss this issue; S377 had originally outlawed homosexuality in India, and while its recent repeal is seen as a jurisprudential win for gay rights, homophobic social norms persist. To call something ‘PUBLIC’ entails a sense of shared community. We always strive to foster inclusion and respectful dialogue. We believe that diverse, inclusive, and community-oriented projects yield the strongest results for the individuals and the community as a whole.

We are always seeking for contributors to promote our mission. Each content submission is translated to a graphic design apparel and are sold together in a pair.

Since our platform is built on collaboration, we believe in profit sharing. All collaborators will get a share profit from each product they contributed to. This means that more sales will equal more profit for each collaborator!

Anyone from any background can be a contributing writer as long as you produce a piece that is related to PUBLIC and fits within the general guidelines of this section.

Each content submission pieces should relate to how the public arena is affected by the current state of affairs in the world. We encourage contributors to submit a broad variety of topics, and each topic with be reviewed by the editorial team.

The best works often examines a microcosm (i.e., a case study or individual’s narrative) and connects this story to the broader role of systems, institutions, and macrocosmic forces that cause them. The content can derive from personal experience or entirely research-based as long as it provides a balanced, critical angle and supporting facts.

Each written piece should generally involve 400-1,200 words, but there are no strict constraints regarding form or length. This can be done as a written essay, photo essay, or a research paper. Non-traditional, experimental forms will also be considered.

Our tone of voice is educated, grounded, and irreverent. Any piece of PUBLIC content is diligent, well- researched, and thorough; critical thought is at the epicenter of PUBLIC’s identity. We are knowledgeable without the pomp and pretense of academia. Our platform is constructive and critical, and we take each in proper measure, although there are times when you need to fuck something up. We are not afraid to be provocative or controversial when the circumstances demand.

As each design is paired with a submission, the goal of the merchandise design is to enhance the core messages of the article by explaining key concepts visually. Utilitarian and brutalist styles should drive the merchandise design; functionality should precede aesthetics. Each element on the merch must serve a purpose and communicate ideas from the article in a visual form. Designers are encouraged to push the boundaries but maintain PUBLIC’s personality.
